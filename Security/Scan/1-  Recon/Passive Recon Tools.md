# Email Discovery & Validation
## **1. Bugcrowd Engagements**

**Purpose:**  
A directory of real bug-bounty programs and their scopes.  
**Use in Passive Recon:**

- Helps you understand what domains/assets you are allowed to investigate.
    
- Useful before doing any recon so you stay within legal boundaries.  
    **Why it's important:**  
    Reconfirm scope before performing passive recon on any target.
    

---

## **2. Hunter.io**

**Purpose:**  
One of the most popular OSINT tools for discovering and analyzing email address patterns.  
**What it does:**

- Finds public email addresses associated with a domain.
    
- Detects naming patterns (e.g., firstname.lastname@domain).
    
- Shows sources where emails were found.
    
- Includes an email verification service.  
    **Why it's useful:**  
    Helps reveal structure of company emails quickly and gives a strong starting point for mapping personnel.
    

---

## **3. IntelX (Intelligence X)**

**Purpose:**  
A deep OSINT search engine for leaked data, breaches, pastebins, documents, and historic information.  
**What it does:**

- Finds emails leaked in old dumps or public paste sites.
    
- Uncovers hidden or older email formats that are no longer publicly visible.
    
- Searches across darknet, public web, and archived datasets.  
    **Why it's useful:**  
    Excellent for discovering emails not visible through normal public searches — especially useful for analyzing breach exposure.
    

---

## **4. Email Hippo (tools.emailhippo.com)**

**Purpose:**  
A professional-grade email address validation tool.  
**What it does:**

- Checks whether an email is deliverable.
    
- Analyzes MX records.
    
- Detects catch-all domains.
    
- Confirms whether mailbox likely exists without sending email.  
    **Why it's useful:**  
    Great for filtering out invalid or fake emails when building a clean list from recon.
    

---

## **5. Email-Checker.net**

**Purpose:**  
A simple and fast email verification service.  
**What it does:**

- Checks syntax, MX records, and deliverability.
    
- Good for quick checks during recon.  
    **Why it's useful:**  
    Lightweight, easy-to-use validator to confirm whether guessed or discovered emails might be active.

## **6. Emailnator**

**Purpose:**  
A service that provides **temporary disposable Gmail addresses**.

**What it does:**

- Generates random Gmail-based temporary inboxes.
    
- Helps receive verification codes or view emails without exposing your real address.
    
- Useful for OSINT tasks requiring sign-ups or previews while keeping anonymity.
    

**Why it’s useful in passive recon:**

- Lets you create accounts on recon tools or test platforms without using personal email.
    
- Helps avoid linking your identity to research activities.
    
- Useful for accessing sites requiring email verification while maintaining privacy.
